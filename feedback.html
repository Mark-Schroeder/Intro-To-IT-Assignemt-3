Emre Altunsu

Reflection: What went well?

Throughout the completion of assignment 3, a clear and distinct chemistry strengthen between group members. This exceled the groups ability to complete task components, collaborate effectively and prioritize time management over all else. As a group, we demonstrated high levels of determination in ensuring each group member felt supported, comfortable (to some extent) and understood the current and/or future objectives in general.

Reflection: What could be improved?

Although we worked quite well as a team in most aspects, potential downfalls were most apparent in independent activities. The group was capable of completing assigned components, but awareness between ‘what was completed’ and ‘what was in progress’ may have slightly reduced work proficiency, however Jacks implementation of agendas and roles mitigated a majority of these issues.

An obvious point of surprise was in the teams ability to work effectively, but most oddly our ability to act as a sort of ‘support group’ was actively highlighted. Each individual group member faced some sort of challenge throughout the length of the assignment. However, it was our teams ability to share this information, address it and form efficient solutions which ultimately propelled us forward with intensified determination.

Reflection: One thing we learned about groups

Although the majority of group members established their experience working in team-based environments in both a professional and social sense, assignment 3 has really heightened the importance of having a supportive and efficient team. This may not always be the case, so being able to adjust your groups processes and motivations can really draw the line between proficient team work and a disorganized group of individuals.

It was established early in assignment 3, that skill levels were heavily skewed between group members. Some individuals finding topics easy, while others constantly struggle to understand specific concepts. Each individual group member at some point has highlighted that they are most certainly capable of utilizing important tools such as Github in various instances. Our current implementation of Github ‘Commit Trails’ have fortified the idea of purpose and objective in and around the planning and potential production of the ‘Reflections’ application

What went well?

The collaboration was outstanding, I had never been in a group who wanted to actively maximise their learning and delivery targets. One thing we learned about groups

Agile’s software development manifesto provided myself with a new perspective on both group work and project delivery. I truly believe that constant interactions, engaging collaboration, responding to change, and working software are fun principles to encourage teams to work together, especially if it means avoiding comprehensive documentation that is boring and inevitable to change.

Tools
