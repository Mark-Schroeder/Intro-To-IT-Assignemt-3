Overall Group Reflection

What went well? 

We at Kaleid AI collectively agree that we worked outstandingly well with one another, emphasising the amount of hard work and collaboration each member put into their sections. Everyone identified that communication and cooperation were also of an exceptionally high standard, while also voicing how each felt supported, encouraged, comforted, and determined by the group’s attitude, communication, and quality of work. Participation and interactivity were also noticed across our team and were highly credited across deeper reflection. Time management and task allocation were also seen as one of the more highly valued attributes of our team, especially when it came to setting agendas and roles withinside meetings. We collectively worked together to create artefacts that represented our project idea well, while also discussing and analysing aspects of technology and careers inside the reporting basis, splitting the load equally, while also aiming for this equality to suit one’s interests. We believe as a team that this effort has been widely shown throughout this project and these artefacts and has empowered us to envision a further prospect of ‘Reflections’.

What could be improved? 

Even with all the positive aspects listed above, we know that reflecting even on the improvement side of things is a crucial aspect to analyse and understand what could have been done better. Most of the discussion of improvements revolved around the lack of strategy earlier on in the project, and the lack of specific clarity to do with independent activities. Withinside Assignment 2, we identified a lack of strategy and leadership in our team dynamic, with more introverted personalities being present. We quickly realised that we needed to consolidate a solution to this issue, so cohesive agendas and planning were created to mitigate this by Jack, which everyone commented positively on. However, we agree that this solution was perhaps too late in implementation and that we should have devised a solution quicker to increase overall proficiency. Otherwise, we all agree that we worked quite well together throughout these assignments, though thought into further developing a much more interactive prototype may also be considered for improvement.

One thing surprising? 

Each of us agreed that the collaboration and support of everyone within this team was the most surprising and accepted factor of this experience, while also empowered by the complementation of skills and personalities that fit incredibly well together to make this team what it was. The support aspect of this group was one of the most appraised aspects of surprise, with each member complimenting on their experiences with difficult circumstances being mitigated inside this team and identifying this as a ‘safe space’ for ideas, difficulties, and challenges to be brought up to light to be discussed, mitigated, and solved. With great effort too, our team demonstrated their skill in rapid and agile developmental skills, creating detailed artefacts in a quite short amount of time, while also showing grand capability in creating a highly detailed report also.

One thing we learnt about teams?

Communication and its benefits in a team environment were clearly identified across deeper reflection. Aspects of support, interactions, collaboration, and organisation made up what we believed to be the key parts of this communication, strung together by like-minded individuals targeted towards a common goal. Withinside this clear-cut communication, we identified that a group's attitude, motivation, and effectiveness can be established so that all in the team can benefit from such, as they individually connect with that goal and with the team itself. We agree that collaboration and communication inside a group environment are crucial underlying factors of any group, and we identify that we indeed found this to be true in our team, as we cohesively and effectively split work between us, aiming to best suit everyone’s strengths and capabilities, while also understanding when to step in and help.

Tools

It was identified by our group that the main way of establishing tools was through the agendas sorted out during weekly meetings, which clearly identified what everyone is to work on for that period. Since specific skill sets may have been better experienced by someone else, we organised the team a such to best complement each’s ability, leaving some contributions made to be technically incorrect, as components were constantly modified by other members of the group. To achieve effectiveness and clarity, only 2-3 people were assigned to commit to GitHub for this assignment, as having more may have proved more difficult otherwise. In order to be consistent, we used small groups of teams focusing on varieties of aspects, such as ‘Development’ and ‘Testing’ to truly cover all the bases for this assignment, sorting out specific tools withinside these groups to make the most of everyone’s time.

Conclusion

Overall, we believe that our team has gone above and beyond what we originally envisioned, empowered with both support and engagement from the entire team. With collaboration and clear communication, we were able to achieve putting together many artefacts that demonstrated our vision, while also providing a detailed report with valuable and insightful information. Although the beginning of this journey may have been a bit rocky due to limited strategic and leadership capabilities, we all agree that we have consolidated an incredible group dynamic, capable of achieving and overcoming any challenge in our way, thanks to the overall supportive nature of our group, plus the additional methods put in place to best work as a cohesive and collective group.
